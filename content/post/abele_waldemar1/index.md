On February 10, 1932, Abele Waldemar of Baden, near Vienna, patented a single-runner ski sled paired with two short skis worn on the rider’s feet. The design improved control and safety on icy or sloped tracks, allowing smooth steering and braking even at high speeds.

Date of Application: 18 February 1930

Date of Issuance: 10 February 1932
